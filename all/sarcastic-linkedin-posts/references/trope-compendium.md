# LinkedIn Trope Compendium

The raw material. Satire is only as good as its source fidelity — imitate these precisely.

## The Broetry Skeleton

The signature LinkedIn format ("broetry"): a vertically stretched poem of one-sentence lines designed to maximize scroll depth and dwell time.

Canonical structure, top to bottom:

1. **Clickbait hook** — one dramatic line, cut off by "…see more" ("I got rejected 47 times." / "I fired my top performer yesterday.")
2. **Scene-setting fragments** — short lines painting past hardship or the inciting incident. No paragraphs. Dramatic pauses via line breaks.
3. **The turn** — "Then it hit me." / "One day, everything changed." / "That's when a stranger tapped my shoulder."
4. **The rise** — compressed success montage, often as a ✅ checklist with metrics.
5. **The fortune-cookie moral** — one aphorism. ("Grit beats talent every time.")
6. **Direct address to the struggling reader** — "If you're going through this right now — I see you. Keep going. 🙏"
7. **Engagement bait** — a question or a "comment WORD for the guide" CTA, usually with 👇.
8. **Hashtag cluster** — 4–6, generic: #leadership #growthmindset #hustle.
9. **Optional P.S.** — the sales funnel reveal.

## Post Archetypes (pick 1 primary, ≤2 secondary)

- **The Humblebrag** — modesty as delivery mechanism for bragging. "I never thought a kid from [humble origin] would one day [impressive thing]. Grateful doesn't even cover it. 🙏" Pair with photo-of-award energy.
- **Hustle Porn / Grindset** — overwork as moral virtue. 4 AM alarms, cold plunges, "no days off," sleep framed as a character flaw. Tough-love register: "Nobody is coming to save you."
- **Fake Vulnerability** — opens with struggle (burnout, layoff, rejection), pivots within two lines to a lesson, a brand, or a triumph arc. The vulnerability is a hook, never a wound.
- **The Milestone Announcement** — "🚀 Thrilled, humbled, and beyond excited to announce…" Tags a dozen people. Confetti energy. Works for jobs, certifications, or (satirically) anything: a gym membership, a new stapler.
- **The Contrarian Take** — "Unpopular opinion: [conventional wisdom] is dead." The take is either obvious, walked back by paragraph three, or secretly self-serving.
- **The Numbered Framework** — "7 traits of exceptional leaders:" followed by traits that apply to any mammal. Bold headers, generic content, "steal this."
- **The Screenshot Post** — a text/Slack/email screenshot (described, in our case) + a moral about kindness or resilience. The screenshot always flatters the poster.
- **The Founder Origin Story** — "Two years ago I was fired. Today we crossed $1.3M ARR." Stripe dashboard energy. The firing is always the best thing that ever happened.
- **The Recruiter Post** — exclamation-point-dense hiring announcement. "Tag someone who'd be a great fit!"
- **The Value Bomb / DM Funnel** — "Steal my exact system." Carousel framing. Ends with "DM me 'GUIDE'."
- **The Stranger Encounter** — an ordinary interaction (barista, Uber driver, child, pigeon) mined for profound business insight. The stranger sometimes turns out to be a CEO/hiring manager. This is the purest form of the genre and the most satirizable.

## Stock Vocabulary (deploy 3–5 per post, verbatim)

Openers and connective tissue:

- "Thrilled / humbled / honored / beyond excited to announce…"
- "I'm not going to sugarcoat this…"
- "Let that sink in."
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Repeat after me:"
- "This might ruffle some feathers, but…"
- "Read that again."
- "Grateful doesn't begin to cover it."

Buzzword bank:
grit, hustle, authenticity, servant leadership, growth mindset, 10x, unlock, north star, level up, circle back, double-click on that, radical candor, stakeholder alignment, synergy (ironic-only in the wild; fair game for satire).

Fortune-cookie morals (model these, invent fresh ones):

- "Leadership isn't a title. It's a mindset."
- "Culture eats strategy for breakfast."
- "Rejection isn't the end of your story. It's the plot twist."

Engagement closers:

- "Thoughts?" / "Agree?" / "Am I wrong?"
- "Who else has felt this?"
- "Curious what others think 👇"
- "Comment 'YES' if you agree." (Note: real LinkedIn now penalizes this as engagement bait — which makes it funnier to use in satire, the trope fossilizing in real time.)
- "Repost ♻️ if this resonated."

## Formatting Conventions

- **Emoji as bullets**: 🚀 launches/growth, 🙏 gratitude, 💡 ideas, ✅ achievement lists, 👇 pointing at bait, ♻️ repost requests. Budget: 3–6 per post.
- **Exclamation inflation** — mundane updates get multiple exclamation points ("Renewed my certification!!").
- **Mid-sentence emphasis** — bold or ALL CAPS on the wrong word for maximum fake gravitas.
- **The ✅ list** — three items: two plausible, one absurd, OR escalating absurdity (✅ 7-figure agency ✅ Team of 12 ✅ Featured in Forbes).
- **No external links in the body** — the genre superstition that links kill reach. Satirically useful: "Link in comments (the algorithm made me do it)."

## Comment-Section Culture (for satirizing replies or writing fake comments)

- Low-effort affirmations: "Great post!" "So true!" "This 💯" — from people who read only the hook.
- Engagement pods — coordinated early likes/comments to game velocity. Satirical gold: the suspiciously instant "Powerful stuff, [FirstName]!" 40 seconds after posting.
- The tag reflex — "@Jessica this is so you."
- Thread hijacking — "Love this! Speaking of leadership, I help founders scale — sent you a DM!"
- Hedged disagreement — "Great points, and I don't disagree, but have you considered…" (Open conflict is genre-forbidden; even fake dissent grovels first.)

## Profile-Voice Details (for personas)

- Headline stacking: "CEO | Speaker | 3x Author | Forbes 30U30 | Helping Founders Scale to 7 Figures | Dad."
- Second-person About sections: "Are you a founder stuck at $10K MRR? I help you break through."
- Banner logos, "Featured in" walls, pinned top-performing posts as social proof.

## Why the Genre Exists (useful for Savage mode)

Satire lands harder when it hits the incentive, not just the surface:

1. **Algorithm worship** — dwell time and early comment velocity drive reach, so the form optimizes for scroll-stretching and bait. (The vertical poem exists because the ranking system rewards it, not because anyone enjoys reading it.)
2. **Professional-persona pressure** — real names + employers attached to every post ⇒ compulsory positivity, gratitude theater, conflict aversion.
3. **The personal-brand economy** — most power-posters are selling something; every story is a funnel wearing a trench coat.
4. **Meme diffusion** — proven formats get photocopied until the genre satirizes itself ("I proposed to my girlfriend — here's what it taught me about B2B sales").
5. **Backlash saturation** — the genre is now self-aware; sincere and parody posts are indistinguishable. Your satire lives in that ambiguity. Use it.

## 2026 Meta-Layer (optional seasoning)

The platform now reportedly penalizes the classic tropes: engagement-bait phrasing is detected and suppressed, engagement pods get shadowbanned, one-liner platitude formatting is deprioritized in favor of "knowledge value," and bait-and-switch hooks are punished via click-bounce tracking. For satire, this enables a delicious new register: **the thought-leader in decline** — a narrator whose reach is dying and who pivots to "authentic long-form value" with exactly the same hollow soul. ("The algorithm changed. I changed with it. Here's my 2,000-word essay on why my dog's groomer understands B2B attribution.")
